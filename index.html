Why the Hunter Valley?

Hunter Valley, the Heart of Wine and Wonder

The Hunter Valley, in New South Wales, Australia, is a viticultural gem known for its world-class wineries and landscapes. As one of the country's oldest wine regions, it boasts a rich winemaking heritage since the early 19th century. With over 150 wineries, producing renowned Semillon and Shiraz, visitors can enjoy tastings, tours, and gourmet dining amid rolling hills and lush vineyards, making it a top destination for wine lovers and history buffs.

Beyond its wine culture, the Hunter Valley offers diverse activities. Its natural beauty shines in parks like Watagans and Wollemi, perfect for hiking and wildlife spotting. The Hunter Valley Gardens provide a peaceful retreat, while charming towns offer boutique shopping and artisanal food. Whether exploring the vibrant food scene or relaxing in a luxury spa, the Hunter Valley's natural splendor and cultural richness ensure an unforgettable experience.

Audrey Wilkinson

Experience the breathtaking beauty of the Hunter Valley from atop the Audrey Wilkinson hill, where each sip of wine tells a story of passion and craftsmanship.

Address

750 De Beyers Rd, Pokolbin NSW 2320

What I like about it

What captivates me about Audrey Wilkinson Vineyard is not only their stunning panoramic views but also their dedication to producing handcrafted wines that showcase the region's unique terroir.

What I like about Tyrrell's Wines is their commitment to preserving traditional winemaking methods while also embracing innovation. This resultes in wines of unparalleled quality and distinction for people now and in the future to enjoy.
